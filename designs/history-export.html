Prac.

Your record, and getting it out safely.

A filterable history of everything logged, and an export framed as a student-owned reflective record — not assessment evidence. The export runs through an identifier-review gate the student can't skip. On-device for v1: nothing leaves the phone, and the copy never claims the text is “safe” — only that common identifiers were flagged for review.

“4 identifiers fixed,” not “de-identified.” The number is concrete and honest; the warning sits on the success screen precisely where confidence is highest and a breach is most likely.

What this build commits to. Export is a student-owned reflective record, never framed as assessment evidence. The identifier check is a mandatory gate between “choose scope” and “file exists” — structural, not a setting. Every flag forces a human decision (fix or dismiss); the copy stays in coaching register and never asserts the text is safe or de-identified. Flags raised while writing persist in the history and detail views, so confidentiality is a running habit, not a final hurdle. All of it runs on-device for v1 — no clinical text leaves the phone.

The honest limits of v1, on the record. A client-side heuristic has real recall gaps: it will miss unstructured identifiers (“the only patient of X background that week,” rare presentations, indirect references). The design mitigates this by refusing to claim completeness and by forcing review — but the residual risk is real, and the share step is outside the app’s control entirely. If you ever want higher recall, that means a model with better language understanding, which reopens the “does text leave the device” question you’ve closed for v1.

Still unbuilt, and still the bigger lever. None of this answers whether students finish the core flow twice. A working export for a corpus nobody’s filling is polish on an empty record — the completion test still outranks everything here.
